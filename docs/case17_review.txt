Exhale Academy CSE Review
Case 17 -- Trauma Critical (Hemothorax)

Intro
Chest trauma with progressive dyspnea and suspected pleural blood accumulation requiring drainage-first management.

Stem
Trauma patient has dyspnea and worsening oxygenation after blunt chest injury.

Step 1
A 47-year-old woman is brought to the emergency department after blunt chest trauma.

While receiving O2 by nonrebreathing mask, the following are noted:
HR 126/min
RR 34/min
BP 102/64 mm Hg
SpO2 84%

Which of the following should be evaluated initially? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 3).

A. Inspect chest expansion (+2)
B. Auscultate breath sounds (+2)
C. Percuss the chest (+2)
D. Obtain bedside spirometry (-3)
E. Delay treatment until a complete imaging workup is finished (-3)

Revealed findings after indicated selections:
- Chest expansion is decreased on the left.
- Breath sounds are decreased on the left.
- Percussion is dull on the left.

High-score progression: Left chest expansion is reduced. Breath sounds are decreased on the left, and percussion is dull on the left.
Default progression: Assessment is incomplete. Hypoxemia and blood-loss risk worsen.

Step 2
Dyspnea and hypoxemia persist, and pleural blood is suspected. Which of the following should be recommended FIRST?

A. Administer high-concentration oxygen and begin pleural drainage (+3)
B. Provide analgesia only and reassess later (-3)
C. Delay drainage until symptoms worsen further (-3)
D. Start bronchodilator therapy (-3)

High-score progression: Breath sounds improve slightly, and oxygenation begins to increase.
Default progression: Pleural blood continues to impair ventilation and oxygenation.

Step 3
After pleural drainage is started, oxygenation improves.

While receiving O2 by nonrebreathing mask, the following are noted:
HR 114/min
RR 28/min
BP 110/70 mm Hg
SpO2 90%

Which of the following should be evaluated now? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 4).

A. Reassess breath sounds (+2)
B. Check chest-tube output for ongoing bleeding (+2)
C. Trend oxygen saturation and blood pressure (+2)
D. Determine whether repeat imaging is needed after stabilization (+2)
E. Routine discharge paperwork (-3)
F. Stop close monitoring after the first improvement (-3)

Revealed findings after indicated selections:
- Breath sounds are louder on the left than before.
- Chest-tube output should be followed closely for ongoing blood loss.
- Oxygenation and blood pressure are improving but still require close monitoring.
- Repeat imaging may still be needed after stabilization.

High-score progression: Drainage response is present, but continued monitoring is required.
Default progression: Reassessment is delayed, and recurrent instability develops.

Step 4
The patient remains at risk for recurrent bleeding and respiratory deterioration. Which of the following should be recommended now?

A. Continue monitored trauma care with chest-tube management and ICU-level reassessment (+3)
B. Transfer to an unmonitored floor bed (-3)
C. Discharge after oxygenation improves briefly (-3)
D. Stop oxygen because drainage has started (-3)

High-score progression: Final outcome: hemothorax is treated with pleural drainage and continued high-acuity monitoring.
Default progression: Final outcome: inadequate monitoring leads to recurrent deterioration.
