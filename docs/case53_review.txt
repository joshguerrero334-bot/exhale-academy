Exhale Academy CSE Review
Case 53 -- Adult Medical Critical (Thoracic Surgery Post-Op Complications)

Step 1
A 67-year-old man is in the ICU the day after thoracic surgery and develops increasing dyspnea.

While receiving O2 by nasal cannula at 4 L/min, the following are noted:
HR 126/min
RR 30/min
BP 134/78 mm Hg
SpO2 85%
EtCO2 49 mm Hg

Which of the following should be evaluated initially? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 4).

Options
A. Auscultate breath sounds
B. Inspect the chest-tube system
C. Review the chest radiograph
D. Obtain an ABG
E. Review the CBC
F. Delay treatment until the full diagnostic panel is completed
G. Transfer to a low-acuity bed before stabilization

Step 2
Which of the following should be recommended FIRST?

Options
A. Provide oxygen, optimize pain control, begin aggressive pulmonary hygiene, and monitor closely for ventilatory fatigue
B. Use oxygen only and wait for more data before beginning disease-specific therapy
C. Move the patient to an unmonitored area after brief improvement
D. Sedate first and reassess later

Step 3
After initial treatment is started, dyspnea improves only slightly.

While receiving O2 by aerosol mask, the following are noted:
HR 120/min
RR 28/min
BP 128/76 mm Hg
SpO2 88%
EtCO2 50 mm Hg

Which of the following should be evaluated now? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 4).

Options
A. Review the repeat chest radiograph
B. Reassess the chest-tube function
C. Repeat the ABG
D. Determine whether bronchoscopy or reintubation is now required
E. Stop close monitoring after the first response to treatment
F. Plan discharge if oxygenation improves briefly

Step 4
Which of the following should be recommended now?

Options
A. Continue high-acuity care and escalate to bronchoscopy or ventilatory support if airway clearance fails
B. Continue the same therapy unchanged and reassess much later
C. Transfer to an unmonitored bed
D. Discharge after transient improvement
