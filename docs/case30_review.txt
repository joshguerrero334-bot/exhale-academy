Exhale Academy CSE Review
Case 30 -- Neuromuscular Critical (Tetanus Airway Risk)

Intro
Puncture-wound history with trismus, rigidity, and rising airway-protection risk.

Stem
Patient develops trismus, rigidity, and worsening respiratory difficulty after a contaminated wound.

Step 1
A 43-year-old man comes to the emergency department because of progressive difficulty opening his mouth and swallowing 4 days after stepping on a nail.

While breathing room air, the following are noted:
HR 122/min
RR 30/min
BP 154/92 mm Hg
SpO2 86%

Which of the following should be evaluated initially? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 4).

A. Assess ability to open the airway and speak clearly (+2)
B. Assess secretion handling, chest wall rigidity, and work of breathing (+2)
C. Obtain an ABG (+2)
D. Review wound history and immunization status (+2)
E. Delay stabilization until culture results return (-3)
F. Assume anxiety is the only cause of respiratory distress (-3)

Revealed findings after indicated selections:
- The jaw is difficult to open, and speech is muffled.
- Neck and abdominal muscles are rigid, and secretions are difficult to clear.
- ABG: pH 7.35, PaCO2 50 torr, PaO2 55 torr, HCO3 27 mEq/L.
- The patient reports no recent tetanus booster after the puncture wound.

Step 2
Findings suggest tetanus with rising airway-protection risk. Which of the following should be recommended FIRST?

A. Provide high-concentration oxygen, reduce stimulation, and prepare for a controlled airway because secretion control is worsening (+3)
B. Give reassurance and observe without airway planning (-3)
C. Delay treatment until spasms stop on their own (-3)
D. Treat with oxygen only and discharge if SpO2 improves briefly (-3)

Step 3
Thirty minutes after oxygen, low-stimulation precautions, and tetanus-directed therapy are started, the patient remains rigid and increasingly fatigued.

While receiving O2 by aerosol mask at an FIO2 of 0.50, the following are noted:
HR 126/min
RR 34/min
BP 162/96 mm Hg
SpO2 88%
EtCO2 52 mm Hg

Which of the following should be evaluated now? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 4).

A. Reassess air movement and chest wall excursion (+2)
B. Reassess airway protection and secretion clearance (+2)
C. Repeat ABG and trend EtCO2 (+2)
D. Determine whether controlled intubation is now required (+2)
E. Stop close monitoring after a small SpO2 improvement (-3)
F. Delay reassessment for several hours (-3)

Revealed findings after indicated selections:
- Air movement is reduced bilaterally because the chest wall is rigid.
- The patient is no longer handling secretions effectively.
- Repeat ABG: pH 7.31, PaCO2 56 torr, PaO2 60 torr, HCO3 28 mEq/L.
- A controlled airway approach is now indicated before complete respiratory failure occurs.

Step 4
Airway protection and ventilation continue to worsen. Which of the following should be recommended now?

A. Proceed with controlled endotracheal intubation and ICU-level ventilatory support (+3)
B. Continue the same treatment unchanged and reassess later (-3)
C. Use CPAP alone and avoid invasive support (-3)
D. Transfer to an unmonitored bed once the patient is calmer (-3)
