Exhale Academy CSE Review
Case 15 -- COPD Critical (High-Risk Airway Pathway)

Intro
Critical COPD exacerbation with secretion burden, mental-status decline, and airway protection risk requiring immediate invasive support.

Stem
Critical COPD exacerbation with airway protection concern and hemodynamic instability.

Step 1
A 59-year-old woman with severe COPD is brought to the emergency department because of worsening dyspnea.

While breathing room air, the following are noted:
HR 132/min
RR 38/min
BP 88/54 mm Hg
SpO2 80%

She is confused, has a weak cough, and has copious secretions.

Which of the following should be evaluated initially? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 3).

A. Mental status and ability to protect the airway (+2)
B. Cough strength and secretion burden (+2)
C. Blood pressure, pulse oximetry, and respiratory rate (+2)
D. Complete pulmonary function testing (-3)
E. Exercise oximetry (-3)

High-score progression: Confusion, secretion burden, and hypotension remain concerning.
Default progression: Assessment is incomplete. Airway protection and gas exchange continue to worsen.

Step 2
ABG analysis reveals:
pH 7.23
PaCO2 72 torr
PaO2 50 torr
HCO3- 29 mEq/L

Which of the following should be recommended FIRST?

A. Proceed with endotracheal intubation and mechanical ventilation (+3)
B. Begin bilevel positive airway pressure (-3)
C. Continue oxygen therapy alone and repeat the ABG later (-3)
D. Administer a sedative before deciding on airway management (-3)

High-score progression: The airway is secured, but severe hypercapnia persists on the initial ABG.
Default progression: Inadequate airway management leads to worsening instability.

Step 3
After endotracheal intubation, the patient is receiving volume-controlled ventilation with the following settings:
VT 430 mL
Rate 20/min
FIO2 0.70
PEEP 8 cm H2O

ABG analysis reveals:
pH 7.21
PaCO2 72 torr
PaO2 58 torr
HCO3- 28 mEq/L

Which of the following should be evaluated or adjusted now? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 3).

A. Repeat ABG analysis after ventilator adjustments (+2)
B. Adjust ventilator rate or tidal volume to improve ventilation (+2)
C. Monitor hemodynamics and secretion clearance (+2)
D. Stop reassessment because the patient is intubated (-3)
E. Plan discharge instructions (-3)

High-score progression: Ventilation improves gradually, but close monitoring is still required.
Default progression: Monitoring and adjustment are insufficient, and gas exchange remains unstable.

Step 4
Repeat ABG analysis after ventilator adjustment reveals:
pH 7.26
PaCO2 64 torr
PaO2 66 torr
HCO3- 28 mEq/L

The patient remains intubated and sedated. Which of the following should be recommended now?

A. Continue invasive ventilatory support with frequent reassessment (+3)
B. Extubate because oxygenation improved (-3)
C. Transition immediately to bilevel positive airway pressure (-3)
D. Transfer to a medical floor (-3)

High-score progression: Invasive ventilatory support continues appropriately, and gas exchange begins to stabilize.
Default progression: Premature de-escalation causes recurrent instability.

Step 5
As oxygenation and ventilation begin to stabilize, which of the following should be evaluated now? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 3).

A. Mental status (+2)
B. Secretion burden and cough effectiveness (+2)
C. ABG trend (+2)
D. Pulmonary rehabilitation schedule (-3)
E. Smoking-cessation readiness (-1)

High-score progression: Mental status, secretion burden, and ABG values continue to improve.
Default progression: Reassessment is incomplete, and the patient remains high risk.

Step 6
The patient continues to require invasive ventilatory support. Which of the following should be recommended postadmission?

A. Admit to the ICU for continued ventilatory management and reassessment (+3)
B. Transfer to an unmonitored floor (-3)
C. Discharge after the next ABG (-3)
D. Observe in the emergency department without ICU admission (-3)

High-score progression: Final outcome: the patient is admitted to the ICU for continued invasive ventilatory support and reassessment.
Default progression: Final outcome: inadequate level of care leads to recurrent critical respiratory failure.
