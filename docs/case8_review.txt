Exhale Academy CSE Review
Case 8 -- Post-Extubation Stridor Respiratory Risk

Intro
Recently extubated adult develops escalating noisy breathing and distress requiring immediate upper-airway rescue decisions.

Stem
Acute upper-airway compromise shortly after extubation.

Step 1
Twenty minutes after extubation, a 58-year-old man develops inspiratory stridor and increasing respiratory distress.

While receiving O2 by aerosol mask at an FIO2 of 0.40, the following are noted:
HR 118/min
RR 32/min
BP 154/90 mm Hg
SpO2 85%

He is anxious and using accessory muscles.

Which of the following should be evaluated initially? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 3).

A. Upper-airway sounds and ability to speak (+2)
B. Work of breathing, pulse oximetry, and vital signs (+2)
C. Immediate availability of airway equipment and backup personnel (+2)
D. Transport for CT imaging before treatment (-3)
E. Sedation without an airway plan (-3)

High-score progression: Stridor persists, and upper-airway distress remains present.
Default progression: Assessment is incomplete. Respiratory distress worsens, and oxygenation falls.

Step 2
Inspiratory stridor persists, and work of breathing is increasing. Which of the following should be recommended FIRST?

A. Administer upper-airway rescue therapy while preparing for reintubation if needed (+3)
B. Observe on low-flow oxygen only (-3)
C. Remove oxygen to assess baseline severity (-3)
D. Delay intervention until a full diagnostic panel is completed (-3)

High-score progression: Stridor improves briefly, but airway risk remains high.
Default progression: Upper-airway obstruction worsens, and respiratory fatigue increases.

Step 3
After initial therapy, stridor improves briefly but returns.

While receiving O2 by aerosol mask at an FIO2 of 0.50, the following are noted:
HR 122/min
RR 34/min
BP 150/88 mm Hg
SpO2 87%

The patient is able to speak only single words.

Which of the following should be evaluated now? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 3).

A. Speech tolerance and stridor intensity (+2)
B. Oxygen saturation and work of breathing trend (+2)
C. Immediate readiness for reintubation (+2)
D. Routine discharge paperwork (-3)
E. Stop monitoring after brief improvement (-3)

High-score progression: Speech tolerance worsens, and stridor remains severe.
Default progression: Reassessment is delayed, and airway compromise worsens rapidly.

Step 4
Respiratory distress worsens, and oxygenation continues to fall. Which of the following should be recommended now?

A. Proceed with controlled reintubation with full airway backup (+3)
B. Continue the same therapy and reassess later (-3)
C. Transport the patient before securing the airway (-3)
D. Deeply sedate the patient without securing the airway (-3)

High-score progression: The airway is secured, and oxygenation improves.
Default progression: Upper-airway obstruction causes severe hypoxemia and worsening instability.

Step 5
After reintubation, the airway is secured and oxygenation improves.

While receiving mechanical ventilation with an FIO2 of 0.60 and PEEP of 5 cm H2O, ABG analysis reveals:
pH 7.29
PaCO2 52 torr
PaO2 72 torr
HCO3- 25 mEq/L

Which of the following should be evaluated or adjusted now? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 3).

A. Verify tube position and secure the airway (+2)
B. Adjust ventilator settings to improve gas exchange (+2)
C. Continue close cardiorespiratory reassessment (+2)
D. Stop monitoring after the first improved saturation reading (-3)
E. Transfer immediately to a low-acuity unit (-3)

High-score progression: Post-intubation stabilization is maintained with close monitoring.
Default progression: Monitoring and ventilator-management gaps leave recurrent instability risk.

Step 6
The patient remains at risk for recurrent airway compromise. Which of the following should be recommended postadmission?

A. Admit to the ICU for continued airway and ventilatory monitoring (+3)
B. Discharge after temporary stabilization (-3)
C. Admit to an unmonitored bed (-3)
D. Observe without a defined escalation plan (-3)

High-score progression: Final outcome: the patient is admitted to the ICU for continued airway and ventilatory management.
Default progression: Final outcome: the level of care is inadequate, and recurrent airway distress occurs.
