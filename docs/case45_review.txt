Exhale Academy CSE Review
Case 45 -- Neonatal Critical (Congenital Heart Defect Cyanotic Shunt)

Intro
Neonate with persistent cyanosis and poor oxygen response requiring structural-cardiac recognition and monitored stabilization.

Stem
Neonate has persistent cyanosis despite supplemental oxygen and shows concern for ductal-dependent cardiac disease.

Step 1
A 2-day-old neonate remains cyanotic despite supplemental oxygen.

The following are noted:
HR 166/min
RR 52/min
SpO2 76%

Which of the following should be evaluated initially? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 4).

A. Assess perfusion, murmur, and cyanosis severity (+2)
B. Compare preductal and postductal oxygenation when available (+2)
C. Determine whether oxygen response is disproportionately poor for the level of respiratory effort (+2)
D. Arrange urgent echocardiography and supporting data (+2)
E. Delay stabilization until all diagnostics are complete (-3)
F. Assume every neonatal respiratory problem follows the same pathway (-3)

Revealed findings after indicated selections:
- A murmur is present, and perfusion is borderline.
- Preductal and postductal oxygenation should be compared.
- Poor oxygen response raises concern for a cyanotic structural lesion rather than primary lung disease.
- Echocardiography is needed urgently.

Step 2
Findings suggest cyanotic congenital heart disease. Which of the following should be recommended FIRST?

A. Stabilize oxygenation and perfusion while arranging urgent cardiology-directed evaluation and ductal-dependent management planning (+3)
B. Use oxygen only and defer definitive disease-specific management (-3)
C. Delay escalation planning until prolonged deterioration occurs (-3)
D. Transfer to lower-acuity care after brief early improvement (-3)

Step 3
After initial stabilization, cyanosis persists and the infant remains under close monitoring.

Current findings are:
HR 160/min
RR 50/min
SpO2 78%

Which of the following should be evaluated now? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 4).

A. Trend oxygenation pattern and perfusion findings (+2)
B. Continue comparing data that support a structural-cardiac pattern (+2)
C. Determine whether cardiology-directed ICU care remains necessary (+2)
D. Use blood-gas and echo-directed data to support the ongoing plan (+2)
E. Stop close monitoring after one modest improvement (-3)
F. Delay reassessment for several hours (-3)

Revealed findings after indicated selections:
- Persistent hypoxemia despite oxygen continues to support a cardiac shunt pattern.
- Preductal and postductal data remain important.
- Definitive echocardiographic and cardiology-directed planning is still required.
- ABG may show hypoxemia without a primary ventilatory pattern severe enough to explain the cyanosis.

Step 4
Which of the following should be recommended now?

A. Continue ICU/NICU-level monitored care with urgent cardiology/surgical planning (+3)
B. Transfer to lower-acuity nursery care now (-3)
C. Discharge after temporary stabilization (-3)
D. Observe without explicit escalation triggers (-2)
