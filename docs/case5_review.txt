Exhale Academy CSE Review
Case 5 -- COPD Hypercapnic Respiratory Fatigue

Intro
Adult with severe COPD exacerbation presents with hypoxemia, somnolence, and rising ventilatory fatigue.

Stem
Acute COPD exacerbation with hypercapnia, fatigue, and need for ventilatory support.

Step 1
A 63-year-old woman with severe COPD comes to the emergency department because of worsening dyspnea and productive cough for 2 days.

While breathing room air, the following are noted:
HR 112/min
RR 32/min
BP 156/92 mm Hg
SpO2 84%

She is drowsy and speaks in short phrases.

Which of the following should be evaluated initially? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 3).

A. Mental status and ability to protect the airway (+2)
B. Breath sounds and work of breathing (+2)
C. Pulse oximetry and vital signs (+2)
D. Complete pulmonary function testing (-3)
E. Exercise oximetry (-3)

High-score progression: Breath sounds remain diminished, and the patient remains drowsy with persistent hypoxemia.
Default progression: Assessment is incomplete. The patient becomes more fatigued, and hypoxemia persists.

Step 2
Breath sounds reveal diffuse rhonchi and diminished breath sounds bilaterally. Which of the following should be recommended FIRST?

A. Administer controlled oxygen by Venturi mask (+3)
B. Administer 100% oxygen by nonrebreathing mask (-2)
C. Withhold oxygen because of chronic CO2 retention (-3)
D. Proceed directly to endotracheal intubation (-1)

High-score progression: Oxygenation improves slightly, but dyspnea and somnolence persist.
Default progression: Gas exchange remains unstable, and the patient becomes more somnolent.

Step 3
Thirty minutes after controlled oxygen and bronchodilator therapy are started, the patient remains drowsy.

While receiving O2 by Venturi mask at an FIO2 of 0.28, the following are noted:
HR 108/min
RR 30/min
BP 150/88 mm Hg
SpO2 88%

ABG analysis reveals:
pH 7.28
PaCO2 68 torr
PaO2 56 torr
HCO3- 31 mEq/L

Which of the following should be evaluated now? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 3).

A. Mental status (+2)
B. ABG trend (+2)
C. Secretion burden and cough effectiveness (+2)
D. Cardiopulmonary exercise testing (-3)
E. Discharge planning (-3)

High-score progression: PaCO2 remains elevated, and work of breathing continues to increase.
Default progression: Reassessment is delayed. Somnolence and hypercapnia worsen.

Step 4
The patient becomes more somnolent and continues to use accessory muscles. Which of the following should be recommended now?

A. Begin bilevel positive airway pressure (+3)
B. Continue the same treatment and repeat the ABG later (-3)
C. Discontinue oxygen and observe (-3)
D. Proceed directly to intubation (-1)

High-score progression: The patient becomes more synchronous with ventilatory support, and oxygenation improves.
Default progression: Ventilatory failure worsens, and the patient becomes more difficult to arouse.

Step 5
One hour after bilevel positive airway pressure is started, the patient is receiving IPAP 14 cm H2O, EPAP 6 cm H2O, and FIO2 0.30.

The following are noted:
HR 102/min
RR 26/min
BP 146/84 mm Hg
SpO2 91%

ABG analysis reveals:
pH 7.31
PaCO2 60 torr
PaO2 62 torr
HCO3- 30 mEq/L

Which of the following should be evaluated or adjusted now? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 3).

A. Repeat ABG analysis (+2)
B. Adjust IPAP or backup rate if PaCO2 remains elevated (+2)
C. Check mask fit and patient synchrony (+2)
D. Discontinue bilevel positive airway pressure because SpO2 improved (-3)
E. Reduce oxygen to room air immediately (-2)

High-score progression: pH and PaCO2 begin to improve, and the patient is more alert.
Default progression: Gas exchange remains unstable, and close monitoring is still required.

Step 6
The patient is more alert and synchronizing with bilevel positive airway pressure. Which of the following should be recommended postadmission?

A. Admit to the ICU for continued noninvasive ventilatory management and reassessment (+3)
B. Transfer to an unmonitored medical floor (-3)
C. Discharge home after the next ABG (-3)
D. Observe in the emergency department without admission (-3)

High-score progression: Final outcome: the patient is admitted for ongoing noninvasive ventilatory support and reassessment.
Default progression: Final outcome: inadequate level of care leads to recurrent hypercapnic respiratory failure.
