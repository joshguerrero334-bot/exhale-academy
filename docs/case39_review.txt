Exhale Academy CSE Review
Case 39 -- Pediatric Critical (Cystic Fibrosis Secretion Burden)

Intro
Child with CF has thick secretions, infection burden, and worsening oxygenation/work of breathing.

Stem
Child with chronic productive cough now has heavier secretions and increasing respiratory distress.

Step 1
A 12-year-old girl with cystic fibrosis is brought to the emergency department because of increased cough, thick secretions, and worsening shortness of breath.

While receiving room air, the following are noted:
HR 146/min
RR 38/min
BP 106/66 mm Hg
SpO2 85%

Which of the following should be evaluated initially? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 4).

A. Auscultate breath sounds and assess work of breathing (+2)
B. Assess sputum burden and chronic disease clues (+2)
C. Review chest radiograph findings (+2)
D. Order sputum culture and objective gas-exchange assessment (+2)
E. Delay stabilization while waiting for full diagnostics (-3)
F. Assume all wheezing children should receive the same treatment (-3)

Revealed findings after indicated selections:
- Breath sounds reveal coarse crackles and rhonchi with prolonged exhalation.
- Thick purulent sputum is present, and clubbing is noted.
- Chest radiograph shows hyperinflation with patchy infiltrates.
- Sputum culture is indicated to guide antibiotic therapy.

Step 2
Findings suggest cystic fibrosis exacerbation with secretion burden. Which of the following should be recommended FIRST?

A. Provide oxygen, initiate airway-clearance therapy, and begin culture-directed antimicrobial support (+3)
B. Use oxygen only and defer definitive management (-3)
C. Apply routine bronchodilator and steroid escalation by default (-3)
D. Transfer to a lower-acuity setting after brief improvement (-3)

Step 3
Thirty minutes after initial treatment, the child remains on oxygen and airway-clearance therapy.

Current findings are:
HR 138/min
RR 34/min
BP 104/64 mm Hg
SpO2 90%

Which of the following should be evaluated now? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 4).

A. Reassess breath sounds, secretion clearance, and oxygenation trend (+2)
B. Track culture/infection response (+2)
C. Determine whether airway-clearance and oxygen support remain adequate (+2)
D. Repeat ABG if gas-exchange concern persists (+2)
E. Stop close monitoring after one modest improvement (-3)
F. Delay reassessment for several hours (-3)

Revealed findings after indicated selections:
- Breath sounds and oxygenation improve but remain abnormal.
- Sputum culture and infection response should be followed.
- Airway-clearance effectiveness and secretion burden still require reassessment.
- ABG: pH 7.35, PaCO2 46 torr, PaO2 63 torr, HCO3 25 mEq/L.

Step 4
Which of the following should be recommended now?

A. Continue high-acuity pediatric care with airway-clearance therapy, oxygen, and infection follow-up (+3)
B. Transfer to an unmonitored bed (-3)
C. Discharge after temporary improvement (-3)
D. Observe without explicit escalation triggers (-2)
