Exhale Academy CSE Review
Case 54 -- Adult Neuro Critical (Head Trauma Cheyne-Stokes/ICP)

Intro
Head trauma with declining consciousness, abnormal breathing pattern, and rising ICP concern.

Stem
Trauma patient has worsening mental status, abnormal breathing, and signs of intracranial injury.

Step 1
A 32-year-old woman is brought to the emergency department after a motor vehicle crash.

While receiving O2 by nonrebreathing mask with cervical stabilization in place, the following are noted:
HR 112/min
RR 28/min with an irregular waxing and waning pattern
BP 170/102 mm Hg
SpO2 86%
EtCO2 50 mm Hg

Which of the following should be evaluated initially? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 4).

A. Assess GCS, pupil size, and airway reflexes (+2)
B. Characterize the breathing pattern and look for signs of ICP-related deterioration (+2)
C. Obtain an ABG (+2)
D. Review emergent head imaging after stabilization (+2)
E. Delay stabilization until all imaging is complete (-3)
F. Remove cervical stabilization for easier assessment (-3)

Revealed findings after indicated selections:
- Pupils are unequal, and the GCS is 7.
- Breathing pattern is consistent with Cheyne-Stokes respirations.
- ABG: pH 7.29, PaCO2 54 torr, PaO2 58 torr, HCO3 25 mEq/L.
- Head CT reveals acute intracranial hemorrhage with mass effect.

Step 2
Findings suggest severe head injury with impaired airway protection and hypercapnia. Which of the following should be recommended FIRST?

A. Preoxygenate, intubate, and provide controlled ventilation while avoiding severe hypercapnia (+3)
B. Use oxygen only and delay airway planning (-3)
C. Sedate the patient before securing ventilation strategy (-3)
D. Transfer to lower-acuity care once saturation improves briefly (-3)

Step 3
After initial stabilization, the patient is intubated and receiving volume-controlled ventilation.

Current findings are:
HR 106/min
RR 20/min
BP 160/96 mm Hg
SpO2 92%
EtCO2 38 mm Hg

Which of the following should be evaluated now? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 4).

A. Repeat ABG and trend EtCO2 (+2)
B. Repeat focused neurologic assessment (+2)
C. Confirm ventilator targets are avoiding worsening hypercapnia or excessive hypocapnia (+2)
D. Determine whether ICU-level ICP-focused care remains necessary (+2)
E. Stop close monitoring after one modest improvement (-3)
F. Delay reassessment for several hours (-3)

Revealed findings after indicated selections:
- Repeat ABG: pH 7.37, PaCO2 37 torr, PaO2 74 torr, HCO3 23 mEq/L.
- Pupillary asymmetry persists, and neuro checks remain abnormal.
- Ventilator settings keep EtCO2 near the desired range without severe hypocapnia.
- Ongoing ICP-focused ICU management is still required.

Step 4
Which of the following should be recommended now?

A. Continue ICU-level neurocritical care with controlled ventilation and serial neurologic reassessment (+3)
B. Continue current therapy unchanged and reassess later (-3)
C. Transfer to an unmonitored bed (-3)
D. Discharge after temporary improvement (-3)
