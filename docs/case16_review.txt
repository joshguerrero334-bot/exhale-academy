Exhale Academy CSE Review
Case 16 -- Trauma Critical (Tension Pneumothorax)

Intro
Chest trauma with severe respiratory distress and hemodynamic compromise requiring decompression-first management.

Stem
Trauma patient has severe respiratory distress and hemodynamic instability after chest injury.

Step 1
A 34-year-old man is brought to the emergency department after a motorcycle crash.

While receiving O2 by nonrebreathing mask, the following are noted:
HR 148/min
RR 38/min
BP 78/46 mm Hg
SpO2 78%

Which of the following should be evaluated initially? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 4).

A. Inspect chest expansion (+2)
B. Auscultate breath sounds (+2)
C. Percuss both hemithoraces (+2)
D. Check tracheal position (+2)
E. Obtain bedside spirometry (-3)
F. Delay treatment until chest radiograph is obtained (-3)

Revealed findings after indicated selections:
- Chest expansion is markedly reduced on the right.
- Breath sounds are absent on the right.
- Percussion is hyperresonant on the right.
- The trachea is shifted to the left.

High-score progression: Right chest expansion is reduced. Right breath sounds are absent, percussion is hyperresonant, and the trachea is shifted to the left.
Default progression: Assessment is incomplete. Hemodynamic instability worsens.

Step 2
The patient remains severely hypoxemic and hypotensive. Which of the following should be recommended FIRST?

A. Perform immediate pleural decompression while administering high-concentration oxygen (+3)
B. Obtain a chest radiograph before intervention (-3)
C. Administer analgesia and reassess later (-3)
D. Start bronchodilator therapy (-3)

High-score progression: Right breath sounds improve, and blood pressure increases.
Default progression: Hypoxemia worsens, and shock progresses.

Step 3
After immediate decompression, oxygenation improves and blood pressure increases.

While receiving O2 by nonrebreathing mask, the following are noted:
HR 126/min
RR 30/min
BP 96/60 mm Hg
SpO2 88%

Which of the following should be evaluated now? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 4).

A. Reassess chest expansion (+2)
B. Reassess breath sounds (+2)
C. Trend oxygen saturation and blood pressure (+2)
D. Determine whether chest tube placement is still required (+2)
E. Routine discharge planning (-3)
F. Stop close monitoring after the first improvement (-3)

Revealed findings after indicated selections:
- Right chest expansion has improved.
- Faint breath sounds are now heard on the right.
- Blood pressure and oxygenation are improving but still unstable.
- Definitive chest tube placement is still required.

High-score progression: Right chest expansion and breath sounds improve, but definitive pleural management is still required.
Default progression: Reassessment is delayed, and recurrent deterioration occurs.

Step 4
The patient remains at high risk for recurrent deterioration. Which of the following should be recommended now?

A. Insert a chest tube and admit to the ICU for continued monitoring (+3)
B. Transfer to an unmonitored floor bed (-3)
C. Discharge after oxygenation improves briefly (-3)
D. Observe without definitive pleural management (-3)

High-score progression: Final outcome: tension pneumothorax is treated with decompression, chest tube placement, and ICU care.
Default progression: Final outcome: delayed definitive management leads to recurrent instability.
