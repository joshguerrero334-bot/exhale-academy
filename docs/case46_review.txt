Exhale Academy CSE Review
Case 46 -- Neonatal Critical (BPD Chronic Oxygen Dependence)

Intro
Former preterm infant with chronic lung disease has worsening work of breathing and oxygen need requiring careful support adjustment.

Stem
Former premature infant with chronic oxygen dependence has increased retractions and declining oxygenation.

Step 1
A former 26-week premature infant with chronic oxygen dependence has worsening breathing difficulty.

The following are noted:
HR 162/min
RR 60/min
SpO2 81%

Which of the following should be evaluated initially? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 4).

A. Assess current work of breathing and compare with prior oxygen baseline (+2)
B. Assess chronic lung disease pattern rather than assuming an acute transient problem (+2)
C. Auscultate for crackles, wheezing, and prolonged exhalation (+2)
D. Obtain objective gas-exchange data if oxygen need continues to rise (+2)
E. Delay stabilization until all diagnostics are complete (-3)
F. Assume every neonatal respiratory problem follows the same pathway (-3)

Revealed findings after indicated selections:
- Oxygen requirement has increased above the prior baseline.
- Breath sounds reveal crackles and wheezing with prolonged exhalation.
- A chronic lung disease pattern is present rather than an acute delivery-room problem.
- ABG: pH 7.32, PaCO2 56 torr, PaO2 50 torr, HCO3 28 mEq/L.

Step 2
Findings suggest worsening bronchopulmonary dysplasia. Which of the following should be recommended FIRST?

A. Use the lowest effective oxygen/support strategy and continue NICU-level management while reassessing ventilation and secretion burden (+3)
B. Use oxygen only and defer definitive disease-specific management (-3)
C. Delay escalation planning until prolonged deterioration occurs (-3)
D. Transfer to lower-acuity care after brief early improvement (-3)

Step 3
After initial support adjustment, the infant remains in the NICU.

Current findings are:
HR 156/min
RR 56/min
SpO2 87%

Which of the following should be evaluated now? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 4).

A. Trend oxygen requirement and work of breathing against chronic baseline (+2)
B. Reassess secretion burden and ventilation adequacy (+2)
C. Determine whether current support remains appropriate without over-escalation (+2)
D. Repeat blood gas if oxygenation or ventilation concern persists (+2)
E. Stop close monitoring after one modest improvement (-3)
F. Delay reassessment for several hours (-3)

Revealed findings after indicated selections:
- Oxygen requirement and work of breathing still need trending against baseline.
- Secretion burden and ventilation adequacy still require reassessment.
- Overaggressive oxygen or support should be avoided if a lower effective strategy works.
- ABG: pH 7.34, PaCO2 54 torr, PaO2 56 torr, HCO3 29 mEq/L.

Step 4
Which of the following should be recommended now?

A. Continue NICU-level care using the lowest effective support with structured reassessment (+3)
B. Transfer to lower-acuity nursery care now (-3)
C. Discharge after temporary stabilization (-3)
D. Observe without explicit escalation triggers (-2)
