Exhale Academy CSE Review
Case 6 -- Acute Severe Bronchospasm Fatigue

Intro
Adult with severe lower-airway obstruction presents with hypoxemia, poor speech tolerance, and increasing fatigue.

Stem
Severe bronchospasm with worsening work of breathing and risk of ventilatory failure.

Step 1
A 29-year-old woman comes to the emergency department because of severe dyspnea and chest tightness that began 2 days ago and worsened despite repeated albuterol use.

While breathing room air, the following are noted:
HR 128/min
RR 36/min
BP 168/96 mm Hg
SpO2 82%

She is using accessory muscles and can speak only 2 to 3 words at a time.

Which of the following should be evaluated initially? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 3).

A. Breath sounds and air movement (+2)
B. Ability to speak and accessory-muscle use (+2)
C. Pulse oximetry and vital signs (+2)
D. Methacholine challenge testing (-3)
E. Allergy skin testing before treatment (-3)
F. Complete pulmonary function testing (-3)

High-score progression: Breath sounds reveal diffuse wheezing. Accessory-muscle use remains pronounced, and SpO2 remains low on room air.
Default progression: Assessment is incomplete. The patient becomes more restless, and hypoxemia persists.

Step 2
Breath sounds reveal diffuse expiratory wheezing with prolonged exhalation. Mental status is anxious but alert. Which of the following should be recommended FIRST?

A. Begin continuous nebulized albuterol while administering supplemental oxygen (+3)
B. Obtain a chest CT scan before starting therapy (-3)
C. Proceed directly to endotracheal intubation (-1)
D. Administer a sedative to reduce tachypnea (-3)
E. Withhold oxygen because of concern for CO2 retention (-3)

High-score progression: SpO2 improves slightly, but tachypnea and poor speech tolerance persist.
Default progression: Air movement remains poor, and respiratory distress worsens.

Step 3
Twenty minutes after continuous bronchodilator therapy and systemic corticosteroids are started, the patient remains tachypneic.

While receiving O2 by aerosol mask at an FIO2 of 0.40, the following are noted:
HR 122/min
RR 34/min
BP 160/92 mm Hg
SpO2 90%

She can now speak only 1 to 2 words at a time. Breath sounds are diminished bilaterally with faint wheezing.

ABG analysis reveals:
pH 7.31
PaCO2 52 torr
PaO2 58 torr
HCO3- 25 mEq/L

Which of the following should be evaluated now? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 3).

A. Mental status (+2)
B. Repeat breath sounds and air movement (+2)
C. ABG trend (+2)
D. Routine sputum culture before any further decision (-1)
E. Exercise oximetry (-3)

High-score progression: Air movement decreases further, and the patient becomes more fatigued.
Default progression: Reassessment is delayed. The patient becomes difficult to arouse, and ventilation worsens.

Step 4
Ten minutes later, the patient becomes difficult to arouse. Manual assessment shows very poor air movement.

Repeat ABG analysis reveals:
pH 7.24
PaCO2 61 torr
PaO2 55 torr
HCO3- 26 mEq/L

Which of the following should be recommended now?

A. Proceed with endotracheal intubation and mechanical ventilation (+3)
B. Continue the same treatment and repeat the ABG in 1 hour (-3)
C. Begin noninvasive ventilation (-1)
D. Decrease the FIO2 to reduce oxygen toxicity (-3)

High-score progression: The airway is secured. Oxygenation improves, but hypercapnia persists on the first post-intubation ABG.
Default progression: The patient develops worsening hypoxemia and rising PaCO2.

Step 5
After endotracheal intubation, the patient is receiving volume-controlled ventilation with the following settings:
VT 420 mL
Rate 18/min
FIO2 0.60
PEEP 5 cm H2O

ABG analysis reveals:
pH 7.29
PaCO2 55 torr
PaO2 64 torr
HCO3- 25 mEq/L

Which of the following should be evaluated or adjusted now? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 3).

A. Increase the ventilator rate (+2)
B. Adjust FIO2 to improve PaO2 (+2)
C. Monitor for barotrauma and dynamic hyperinflation (+2)
D. Decrease the ventilator rate to 8/min immediately (-2)
E. Discontinue bronchodilator therapy (-3)

High-score progression: Ventilation becomes easier, and oxygenation begins to improve.
Default progression: Gas exchange remains unstable, and close monitoring is still required.

Step 6
The patient becomes easier to ventilate, and oxygenation begins to improve. Which of the following should be recommended postadmission?

A. Admit to the ICU for continued ventilatory management and reassessment (+3)
B. Transfer to a medical floor once SpO2 reaches 92% (-3)
C. Extubate and discharge when the next ABG improves (-3)
D. Observe in the emergency department without ICU admission (-3)

High-score progression: Final outcome: the patient is admitted to the ICU for ongoing ventilatory support and reassessment.
Default progression: Final outcome: inadequate level of care leads to recurrent respiratory failure.
