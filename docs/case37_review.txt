Exhale Academy CSE Review
Case 37 -- Pediatric Critical (Epiglottitis Sudden Thumb-Sign Emergency)

Intro
Sudden toxic upper-airway emergency with drooling, tripod positioning, and immediate airway risk.

Stem
Child has abrupt fever, drooling, muffled voice, and rapidly worsening airway obstruction risk.

Step 1
A 6-year-old girl is brought to the emergency department because of abrupt fever, drooling, and severe difficulty breathing.

While receiving room air, the following are noted:
HR 156/min
RR 44/min
BP 100/60 mm Hg
SpO2 82%

Which of the following should be evaluated initially? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 4).

A. Assess drooling, voice quality, and ability to handle secretions (+2)
B. Assess stridor severity and overall air entry (+2)
C. Determine whether the child can tolerate routine examination or lying flat (+2)
D. Assess fever and toxic appearance (+2)
E. Delay stabilization while waiting for complete imaging (-3)
F. Assume all pediatric stridor is managed the same way (-3)

Revealed findings after indicated selections:
- Drooling is present, and the voice is muffled.
- Inspiratory stridor is severe, and air entry is limited.
- The child cannot tolerate lying flat or opening the mouth for routine examination.
- High fever and toxic appearance are present.

Step 2
Findings suggest epiglottitis with impending airway obstruction. Which of the following should be recommended FIRST?

A. Keep the child calm, provide blow-by oxygen, and arrange controlled endotracheal intubation with antibiotics after the airway is secured (+3)
B. Use oxygen only and postpone definitive treatment (-3)
C. Force a routine throat exam before airway planning (-3)
D. Transfer to lower-acuity care after a brief improvement (-3)

Step 3
After controlled airway management, the child is receiving mechanical ventilation in the ICU.

Current findings are:
HR 132/min
RR 22/min
BP 104/64 mm Hg
SpO2 96%
EtCO2 36 mm Hg

Which of the following should be evaluated now? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 4).

A. Reassess breath sounds and ventilatory stability (+2)
B. Repeat ABG and review ventilation status (+2)
C. Determine whether airway edema is improved enough for any extubation planning (+2)
D. Continue IV antibiotics and ICU airway monitoring (+2)
E. Stop close monitoring after one modest improvement (-3)
F. Delay reassessment for several hours (-3)

Revealed findings after indicated selections:
- Breath sounds are equal bilaterally, and EtCO2 is acceptable.
- ABG: pH 7.39, PaCO2 38 torr, PaO2 88 torr, HCO3 23 mEq/L.
- Airway edema still requires ICU monitoring before extubation is considered.
- IV antibiotic therapy should continue while the airway stabilizes.

Step 4
Which of the following should be recommended now?

A. Continue ICU-level airway management, ventilation, and antibiotics until edema improves (+3)
B. Transfer to an unmonitored bed (-3)
C. Discharge after temporary improvement (-3)
D. Observe without explicit escalation triggers (-2)
