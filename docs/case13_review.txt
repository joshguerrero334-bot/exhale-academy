Exhale Academy CSE Review
Case 13 -- Asthma Non-Critical (Triggered Exacerbation)

Intro
Noncritical asthma exacerbation after trigger exposure requiring acute bronchodilator treatment, reassessment, and safe discharge planning.

Stem
Triggered asthma exacerbation with wheezing, moderate hypoxemia, and no immediate ventilatory-failure signs.

Step 1
A 43-year-old man comes to the emergency department because of dyspnea, chest tightness, and wheezing after cleaning a dusty attic.

While breathing room air, the following are noted:
HR 118/min
RR 32/min
BP 146/88 mm Hg
SpO2 89%

He has accessory-muscle use and difficulty speaking full sentences.

Which of the following should be evaluated initially? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 3).

A. Breath sounds and air movement (+2)
B. Work of breathing and ability to speak (+2)
C. Pulse oximetry and vital signs (+2)
D. Complete pulmonary function testing (-3)
E. Allergy skin testing before treatment (-3)

High-score progression: Diffuse wheezing persists, and work of breathing remains increased.
Default progression: Assessment is incomplete. Wheezing and dyspnea persist.

Step 2
Breath sounds reveal diffuse expiratory wheezing. Which of the following should be recommended FIRST?

A. Administer oxygen, short-acting bronchodilator therapy, and systemic corticosteroid treatment (+3)
B. Observe without treatment because the patient is still speaking (-3)
C. Start only long-acting controller medication (-3)
D. Proceed directly to intubation (-2)

High-score progression: Oxygenation improves, but wheezing persists.
Default progression: Symptoms improve little, and reassessment remains necessary.

Step 3
Thirty minutes after oxygen, bronchodilator therapy, and systemic corticosteroids are started, the patient is breathing more comfortably but still wheezing.

While receiving O2 by aerosol mask at an FIO2 of 0.35, the following are noted:
HR 108/min
RR 28/min
BP 140/84 mm Hg
SpO2 93%

ABG analysis reveals:
pH 7.45
PaCO2 34 torr
PaO2 68 torr
HCO3- 23 mEq/L

Which of the following should be evaluated now? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 3).

A. Breath sounds and work of breathing (+2)
B. Oxygen saturation and vital-sign trend (+2)
C. Ability to speak and symptom improvement (+2)
D. Peak expiratory flow only after all acute decisions are complete (-1)
E. Discharge paperwork (-3)

High-score progression: Wheezing persists, but the patient remains alert and is improving.
Default progression: Reassessment is delayed, and symptoms persist.

Step 4
Wheezing persists, but the patient remains alert and is speaking in longer phrases. Which of the following should be recommended now?

A. Continue bronchodilator therapy and maintain close reassessment (+3)
B. Stop bronchodilator therapy because the patient is less anxious (-3)
C. Proceed directly to intubation (-3)
D. Delay treatment and reassess tomorrow (-3)

High-score progression: Breathing becomes easier, and symptom improvement continues.
Default progression: Symptoms remain active, and discharge readiness is uncertain.

Step 5
After additional treatment, the patient is breathing more comfortably. Which of the following should be evaluated before disposition? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 3).

A. Sustained symptom improvement and stable oxygen saturation (+2)
B. Inhaler technique and controller adherence plan (+2)
C. Return precautions and follow-up plan (+2)
D. Routine antibiotics without infection evidence (-3)
E. Discharge without trigger-avoidance counseling (-3)

High-score progression: Symptoms remain improved, and discharge planning is complete.
Default progression: Discharge readiness remains incomplete.

Step 6
Symptoms remain improved and oxygenation is stable. Which of the following should be recommended postdischarge?

A. Discharge with an asthma action plan and close follow-up after sustained stability (+3)
B. Admit for monitored care if symptoms worsen again or oxygen needs increase (+1)
C. Discharge without follow-up (-3)
D. Place in hallway observation without a treatment plan (-3)

High-score progression: Final outcome: the patient is discharged with an asthma action plan and follow-up.
Default progression: Final outcome: the discharge plan is inadequate, and symptoms recur early.
