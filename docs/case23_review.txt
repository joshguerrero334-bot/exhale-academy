Exhale Academy CSE Review
Case 23 -- ARDS Critical (Pancreatitis Pathway + Prone Strategy)

Intro
Pancreatitis-triggered lung injury requiring oxygenation rescue planning including prone-position consideration.

Stem
Pancreatitis patient rapidly progresses to severe hypoxemia and rising ventilatory stress.

Step 1
A 35-year-old woman with pancreatitis develops worsening respiratory distress.

While receiving O2 by high-flow nasal cannula at an FiO2 of 0.70, the following are noted:
HR 126/min
RR 33/min
BP 148/86 mm Hg
SpO2 83%

Which of the following should be evaluated initially? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 5).

A. Assess work of breathing and mental status (+2)
B. Review chest radiograph (+2)
C. Obtain ABG and calculate the P/F ratio (+3)
D. Review likely trigger/source data, including appropriate labs (+2)
E. Evaluate whether the pattern is more consistent with noncardiogenic than cardiogenic pulmonary edema (+2)
F. Delay oxygen escalation until all tests return (-3)
G. Assume this is not ARDS because wheezes are not prominent (-2)

Revealed findings after indicated selections:
- Chest radiograph reveals bilateral diffuse infiltrates.
- ABG: pH 7.34, PaCO2 44 torr, PaO2 56 torr, HCO3 24 mEq/L. P/F ratio is severely reduced.
- Lipase is markedly elevated, and CBC shows WBC 18,900/mm3.
- The current findings support noncardiogenic pulmonary edema.

Step 2
Which of the following should be recommended FIRST?

A. Treat the underlying cause and initiate lung-protective ventilation with appropriate PEEP (+3)
B. Use larger tidal volumes to force CO2 clearance (-3)
C. Keep plateau pressure unconstrained if oxygenation is poor (-3)
D. Treat hypoxemia only and ignore the trigger (-2)

Step 3
After initial management, the patient is intubated and receiving lung-protective ventilation.

Current settings are:
AC/VC
VT 6 mL/kg IBW
RR 24/min
FiO2 0.70
PEEP 10 cmH2O
Plateau pressure 28 cmH2O

Which of the following should be evaluated now? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 5).

A. Repeat ABG and reassess the P/F ratio (+2)
B. Trend plateau pressure and compliance (+2)
C. Adjust FiO2 and PEEP based on oxygenation while keeping plateau pressure below 30 cmH2O (+2)
D. Determine whether prone positioning is indicated if severe hypoxemia persists (+2)
E. Follow the trigger-specific source data and hemodynamic response (+1)
F. Stop close monitoring after one slight SpO2 improvement (-3)
G. Delay reassessment for several hours (-3)

Revealed findings after indicated selections:
- ABG: pH 7.31, PaCO2 47 torr, PaO2 68 torr, HCO3 24 mEq/L. P/F ratio remains below 150.
- Plateau pressure remains acceptable, but compliance is poor.
- FiO2/PEEP still require titration based on oxygenation response.
- Prone positioning should be considered if severe hypoxemia persists.
- Pancreatitis management and hemodynamic support still require close follow-up.

Step 4
Which of the following should be recommended now?

A. Continue ICU-level ARDS management with lung-protective ventilation and rescue-escalation criteria (+3)
B. Transfer to a low-acuity floor once oxygenation improves briefly (-3)
C. Discharge after temporary oxygenation improvement (-3)
D. Observe without a structured ARDS escalation plan (-2)
