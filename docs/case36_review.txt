Exhale Academy CSE Review
Case 36 -- Pediatric Critical (Croup Gradual Barking Stridor)

Intro
Gradual viral upper-airway illness with barking cough and stridor at rest.

Stem
Child has progressive barking cough, inspiratory stridor, and increasing work of breathing.

Step 1
A 3-year-old boy is brought to the emergency department because of worsening noisy breathing overnight after several days of upper respiratory symptoms.

While receiving room air, the following are noted:
HR 148/min
RR 42/min
BP 104/64 mm Hg
SpO2 86%

Which of the following should be evaluated initially? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 4).

A. Assess stridor at rest and degree of retractions (+2)
B. Characterize the cough and upper-airway sound pattern (+2)
C. Auscultate air movement and monitor oxygenation (+2)
D. Determine whether drooling or inability to swallow is absent (+2)
E. Delay stabilization while waiting for complete imaging (-3)
F. Assume all pediatric stridor is managed the same way (-3)

Revealed findings after indicated selections:
- There is inspiratory stridor at rest with suprasternal retractions.
- The cough is harsh and barking, and the voice is hoarse.
- Air movement is decreased but breath sounds remain bilateral.
- The child can swallow secretions and is not drooling.

Step 2
Findings suggest moderate-to-severe croup. Which of the following should be recommended FIRST?

A. Administer humidified oxygen, corticosteroid therapy, and racemic epinephrine while observing closely (+3)
B. Use oxygen only and postpone definitive treatment (-3)
C. Force a routine throat exam before airway planning (-3)
D. Transfer to lower-acuity care after a brief improvement (-3)

Step 3
Twenty minutes after initial treatment, the child remains under close observation.

Current findings are:
HR 138/min
RR 36/min
BP 102/62 mm Hg
SpO2 91%

Which of the following should be evaluated now? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 4).

A. Reassess stridor, retractions, and work of breathing (+2)
B. Trend oxygenation and overall clinical response (+2)
C. Determine whether monitored admission is still required (+2)
D. Maintain readiness for airway escalation if stridor at rest worsens again (+2)
E. Stop close monitoring after one modest improvement (-3)
F. Delay reassessment for several hours (-3)

Revealed findings after indicated selections:
- Stridor decreases when calm but returns at rest with agitation.
- Retractions improve but have not resolved completely.
- SpO2 improves with therapy, but monitored observation is still needed.
- Further escalation remains necessary if stridor at rest worsens again.

Step 4
Which of the following should be recommended now?

A. Admit for monitored pediatric high-acuity care until stridor at rest resolves (+3)
B. Transfer to an unmonitored bed (-3)
C. Discharge after temporary improvement (-3)
D. Observe without explicit escalation triggers (-2)
