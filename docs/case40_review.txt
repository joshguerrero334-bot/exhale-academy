Exhale Academy CSE Review
Case 40 -- Pediatric Critical (Foreign Body Aspiration Unilateral Wheeze)

Intro
Toddler with sudden aspiration pattern and unilateral obstruction findings.

Stem
Toddler develops sudden respiratory distress after a choking episode.

Step 1
A 2-year-old girl is brought to the emergency department because of sudden coughing and respiratory distress while eating peanuts.

While receiving room air, the following are noted:
HR 158/min
RR 46/min
BP 98/58 mm Hg
SpO2 81%

Which of the following should be evaluated initially? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 4).

A. Auscultate for unilateral breath-sound reduction (+2)
B. Assess whether wheezing is unilateral rather than diffuse (+2)
C. Clarify the choking-event history (+2)
D. Use imaging only as supportive data, not to rule the diagnosis out (+2)
E. Delay stabilization while waiting for full diagnostics (-3)
F. Assume all wheezing children should receive the same treatment (-3)

Revealed findings after indicated selections:
- Breath sounds are markedly reduced on the right.
- Wheezing is unilateral rather than diffuse.
- The choking episode was witnessed immediately before symptoms began.
- Chest radiograph may be normal if the object is radiolucent.

Step 2
Findings suggest foreign-body aspiration. Which of the following should be recommended FIRST?

A. Arrange urgent rigid bronchoscopy and maintain airway support while awaiting removal (+3)
B. Use oxygen only and defer definitive management (-3)
C. Apply routine bronchodilator and steroid escalation by default (-3)
D. Transfer to a lower-acuity setting after brief improvement (-3)

Step 3
After initial stabilization, the child remains on high-concentration oxygen.

Current findings are:
HR 150/min
RR 40/min
BP 96/58 mm Hg
SpO2 88%

Which of the following should be evaluated now? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 4).

A. Reassess unilateral breath sounds and overall work of breathing (+2)
B. Determine whether imaging limitations still leave bronchoscopy necessary (+2)
C. Confirm that definitive bronchoscopy is still the next step (+2)
D. Repeat ABG if significant respiratory distress persists (+2)
E. Stop close monitoring after one modest improvement (-3)
F. Delay reassessment for several hours (-3)

Revealed findings after indicated selections:
- Unilateral breath-sound reduction persists.
- Radiology still cannot safely exclude a radiolucent airway foreign body.
- Definitive bronchoscopy remains indicated.
- ABG: pH 7.34, PaCO2 47 torr, PaO2 58 torr, HCO3 25 mEq/L.

Step 4
Which of the following should be recommended now?

A. Proceed with definitive bronchoscopy and monitored pediatric care after foreign-body removal (+3)
B. Transfer to an unmonitored bed (-3)
C. Discharge after temporary improvement (-3)
D. Observe without explicit escalation triggers (-2)
