Exhale Academy CSE Review
Case 11 -- COPD Non-Critical (Emphysema-Predominant Flare)

Intro
Noncritical COPD exacerbation with emphysema-predominant findings and persistent dyspnea requiring acute treatment and safe discharge planning.

Stem
Emphysema-predominant COPD exacerbation with moderate hypoxemia and no immediate ventilatory-failure signs.

Step 1
A 67-year-old man with severe COPD comes to the emergency department because of worsening dyspnea after smoke exposure.

While breathing room air, the following are noted:
HR 112/min
RR 30/min
BP 148/92 mm Hg
SpO2 84%

He has pursed-lip breathing and prolonged exhalation.

Which of the following should be evaluated initially? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 3).

A. Breath sounds and air movement (+2)
B. Work of breathing and ability to speak (+2)
C. Pulse oximetry and vital signs (+2)
D. Complete pulmonary function testing (-3)
E. Exercise oximetry (-3)

High-score progression: Pursed-lip breathing and prolonged exhalation persist, and hypoxemia remains present.
Default progression: Assessment is incomplete. Dyspnea and hypoxemia persist.

Step 2
Breath sounds reveal diminished breath sounds bilaterally with scattered wheezes. Which of the following should be recommended FIRST?

A. Administer controlled oxygen and short-acting bronchodilator therapy (+3)
B. Administer 100% oxygen by nonrebreathing mask (-2)
C. Observe without treatment because the patient is still speaking (-3)
D. Proceed directly to endotracheal intubation (-2)

High-score progression: Oxygenation improves slightly, but dyspnea persists.
Default progression: Gas exchange remains unstable, and symptoms worsen.

Step 3
Thirty minutes after controlled oxygen and bronchodilator therapy are started, the patient is less anxious but remains dyspneic.

While receiving O2 by nasal cannula at 2 L/min, the following are noted:
HR 106/min
RR 28/min
BP 144/88 mm Hg
SpO2 89%

ABG analysis reveals:
pH 7.33
PaCO2 56 torr
PaO2 58 torr
HCO3- 29 mEq/L

Which of the following should be evaluated now? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 3).

A. ABG trend (+2)
B. Work of breathing and breath sounds (+2)
C. Oxygen saturation trend (+2)
D. Pulmonary rehabilitation schedule (-3)
E. Smoking-cessation class enrollment before acute treatment decisions (-1)

High-score progression: ABG values and oxygenation remain abnormal, but the patient is still alert.
Default progression: Reassessment is delayed, and dyspnea persists.

Step 4
Dyspnea persists, but the patient is alert and continues to protect the airway. Which of the following should be recommended now?

A. Add systemic corticosteroid therapy and continue bronchodilator treatment (+3)
B. Proceed directly to intubation (-3)
C. Stop bronchodilator therapy and continue oxygen only (-3)
D. Delay additional treatment and reassess in several hours (-2)

High-score progression: Breathing becomes easier, and oxygenation improves.
Default progression: Symptoms persist, and safe discharge remains uncertain.

Step 5
After additional treatment, the patient is speaking in full sentences and oxygenation is improving. Which of the following should be evaluated before disposition? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 3).

A. Sustained symptom improvement and stable oxygen saturation (+2)
B. Inhaler technique and medication adherence (+2)
C. Return precautions and follow-up plan (+2)
D. Routine antibiotics without infection evidence (-3)
E. Discharge without confirming oxygen needs (-3)

High-score progression: Symptom improvement is sustained, and discharge planning is complete.
Default progression: Discharge readiness remains incomplete.

Step 6
Symptoms remain improved and oxygen requirement is stable. Which of the following should be recommended postdischarge?

A. Discharge with follow-up and COPD action planning after sustained stability (+3)
B. Admit for monitored care if oxygen requirement or dyspnea worsens again (+1)
C. Discharge without follow-up (-3)
D. Place in hallway observation without a treatment plan (-3)

High-score progression: Final outcome: the patient is discharged with follow-up and COPD action planning.
Default progression: Final outcome: the discharge plan is inadequate, and symptoms recur early.
