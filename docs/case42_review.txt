Exhale Academy CSE Review
Case 42 -- Neonatal Critical (Meconium Aspiration Vigor/Intubation Threshold)

Intro
Meconium-stained term infant with respiratory distress requiring the correct airway decision without delaying ventilation.

Stem
Newborn delivered through meconium-stained fluid has respiratory distress and uncertain transition stability.

Step 1
A term infant is brought to the warmer after delivery through thick meconium-stained fluid.

The following are noted:
HR 108/min
RR 44/min
SpO2 82%

Which of the following should be evaluated initially? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 4).

A. Assess vigor, respiratory effort, and chest movement (+2)
B. Determine whether meconium is present but ventilation should not be delayed automatically (+2)
C. Auscultate breath sounds and aeration (+2)
D. Obtain objective gas-exchange data if distress is significant (+2)
E. Delay stabilization until all diagnostics are complete (-3)
F. Assume every neonatal respiratory problem follows the same pathway (-3)

Revealed findings after indicated selections:
- The infant is not vigorous and has ineffective respirations.
- Meconium staining is present, but ventilation should not be delayed automatically.
- Breath sounds are coarse bilaterally with poor aeration.
- ABG: pH 7.28, PaCO2 50 torr, PaO2 48 torr, HCO3 23 mEq/L.

Step 2
Findings suggest meconium aspiration with respiratory distress. Which of the following should be recommended FIRST?

A. Provide appropriate respiratory support and proceed with ventilation-focused management rather than delaying for routine suction-only decisions (+3)
B. Use oxygen only and defer definitive disease-specific management (-3)
C. Delay escalation planning until prolonged deterioration occurs (-3)
D. Transfer to lower-acuity care after brief early improvement (-3)

Step 3
Fifteen minutes after initial support, the infant remains under close observation.

Current findings are:
HR 118/min
RR 48/min
SpO2 88%

Which of the following should be evaluated now? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 4).

A. Trend oxygenation, breath sounds, and work of breathing (+2)
B. Use ABG and clinical trend to decide whether ventilation is failing (+2)
C. Determine whether intubation or advanced support is now required (+2)
D. Review radiographic and gas-exchange data if instability persists (+2)
E. Stop close monitoring after one modest improvement (-3)
F. Delay reassessment for several hours (-3)

Revealed findings after indicated selections:
- Repeat oxygenation and air movement should be trended closely.
- ABG may help determine whether ventilation is failing.
- Need for intubation or advanced support should be reassessed if distress persists.
- Chest radiograph may show patchy infiltrates and hyperinflation.

Step 4
Which of the following should be recommended now?

A. Continue NICU-level care with explicit respiratory escalation triggers (+3)
B. Transfer to lower-acuity nursery care now (-3)
C. Discharge after temporary stabilization (-3)
D. Observe without explicit escalation triggers (-2)
