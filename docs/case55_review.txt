Exhale Academy CSE Review
Case 55 -- Adult Neuro Critical (Spinal Injury Airway Stability)

Intro
Cervical trauma with evolving ventilatory weakness and strict immobilization needs.

Stem
Trauma patient has possible high cervical injury with progressive respiratory compromise.

Step 1
A 27-year-old man is brought to the emergency department after a diving accident.

While receiving O2 by nonrebreathing mask with cervical stabilization in place, the following are noted:
HR 108/min
RR 24/min with shallow respirations
BP 122/74 mm Hg
SpO2 87%
EtCO2 45 mm Hg

Which of the following should be evaluated initially? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 4).

A. Assess motor strength, cough strength, and airway protection (+2)
B. Assess breathing pattern, chest expansion, and secretion burden (+2)
C. Obtain an ABG (+2)
D. Review cervical spine imaging while maintaining immobilization (+2)
E. Delay stabilization until all imaging is complete (-3)
F. Remove cervical stabilization for easier assessment (-3)

Revealed findings after indicated selections:
- Motor strength is markedly reduced in both upper and lower extremities.
- Diaphragmatic breathing is weak, and secretion clearance is poor.
- ABG: pH 7.33, PaCO2 50 torr, PaO2 60 torr, HCO3 26 mEq/L.
- Cervical spine imaging shows unstable high cervical injury.

Step 2
Findings suggest high cervical spinal injury with evolving ventilatory weakness. Which of the following should be recommended FIRST?

A. Maintain inline stabilization, provide oxygen, and secure a controlled airway because ventilatory weakness is evolving (+3)
B. Use oxygen only and delay airway planning (-3)
C. Sedate the patient before securing ventilation strategy (-3)
D. Transfer to lower-acuity care once saturation improves briefly (-3)

Step 3
After initial stabilization, the patient remains immobilized and is receiving O2 by face mask.

Current findings are:
HR 104/min
RR 26/min
BP 118/72 mm Hg
SpO2 90%
EtCO2 48 mm Hg

Which of the following should be evaluated now? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 4).

A. Trend VC and repeat ABG (+2)
B. Reassess airway protection and secretion clearance (+2)
C. Verify that inline stabilization is maintained for any airway escalation (+2)
D. Determine whether definitive endotracheal intubation is now required (+2)
E. Stop close monitoring after one modest improvement (-3)
F. Delay reassessment for several hours (-3)

Revealed findings after indicated selections:
- VC is reduced, and inspiratory effort is weakening.
- Repeat ABG: pH 7.30, PaCO2 55 torr, PaO2 62 torr, HCO3 27 mEq/L.
- Inline stabilization remains mandatory during any airway intervention.
- Definitive airway support is now indicated because ventilatory weakness is progressing.

Step 4
Ventilatory weakness continues to worsen. Which of the following should be recommended now?

A. Proceed with controlled endotracheal intubation and ICU-level ventilatory support with spinal precautions (+3)
B. Continue current therapy unchanged and reassess later (-3)
C. Transfer to an unmonitored bed (-3)
D. Discharge after temporary improvement (-3)
