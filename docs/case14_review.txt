Exhale Academy CSE Review
Case 14 -- COPD Critical (NPPV Failure Escalation)

Intro
Critical COPD exacerbation with persistent hypercapnia despite initial therapy and need for timely escalation from bilevel support to intubation.

Stem
Critical COPD exacerbation with worsening ABGs during bilevel support.

Step 1
A 68-year-old man with severe COPD is brought to the emergency department because of worsening dyspnea.

While breathing room air, the following are noted:
HR 126/min
RR 36/min
BP 154/94 mm Hg
SpO2 82%

He is anxious, diaphoretic, and speaking in short phrases.

Which of the following should be evaluated initially? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 3).

A. Breath sounds and air movement (+2)
B. Mental status and ability to speak (+2)
C. Pulse oximetry and vital signs (+2)
D. Complete pulmonary function testing (-3)
E. Exercise oximetry (-3)

High-score progression: Breath sounds remain diminished, and severe dyspnea persists.
Default progression: Assessment is incomplete. Hypoxemia and fatigue worsen.

Step 2
Controlled oxygen, bronchodilator therapy, and IV corticosteroids are started.

ABG analysis reveals:
pH 7.30
PaCO2 64 torr
PaO2 55 torr
HCO3- 30 mEq/L

Which of the following should be recommended now?

A. Begin bilevel positive airway pressure (+3)
B. Continue oxygen therapy alone and repeat the ABG later (-3)
C. Proceed directly to endotracheal intubation (-1)
D. Withhold oxygen because of chronic CO2 retention (-3)

High-score progression: Oxygenation improves slightly, but hypercapnia and tachypnea persist.
Default progression: Respiratory distress continues to worsen.

Step 3
Thirty minutes after bilevel positive airway pressure is started, the patient remains tachypneic.

While receiving IPAP 12 cm H2O, EPAP 5 cm H2O, and FIO2 0.35, the following are noted:
HR 124/min
RR 34/min
BP 150/90 mm Hg
SpO2 88%

Breath sounds remain diminished bilaterally.

Repeat ABG analysis reveals:
pH 7.25
PaCO2 70 torr
PaO2 54 torr
HCO3- 30 mEq/L

Which of the following should be evaluated now? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 3).

A. Mental status (+2)
B. ABG trend (+2)
C. Work of breathing and air movement (+2)
D. Smoking-cessation readiness (-3)
E. Discharge planning (-3)

High-score progression: PaCO2 continues to rise, and mental status worsens.
Default progression: Failure of bilevel support is recognized late, and ventilatory failure worsens.

Step 4
The patient becomes more fatigued and difficult to arouse. Which of the following should be recommended now?

A. Proceed with endotracheal intubation and mechanical ventilation (+3)
B. Continue bilevel support and repeat the ABG later (-3)
C. Decrease the FIO2 (-3)
D. Discontinue bronchodilator therapy (-3)

High-score progression: The airway is secured, and oxygenation begins to improve.
Default progression: The patient develops worsening hypercapnia and hypoxemia.

Step 5
After endotracheal intubation, the patient is receiving volume-controlled ventilation with the following settings:
VT 460 mL
Rate 18/min
FIO2 0.60
PEEP 5 cm H2O

ABG analysis reveals:
pH 7.28
PaCO2 58 torr
PaO2 62 torr
HCO3- 27 mEq/L

Which of the following should be evaluated or adjusted now? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 3).

A. Increase the ventilator rate (+2)
B. Adjust FIO2 to improve oxygenation (+2)
C. Monitor for dynamic hyperinflation and barotrauma (+2)
D. Reduce the ventilator rate to 8/min immediately (-2)
E. Stop reassessing the ABG because the airway is secured (-3)

High-score progression: Ventilation becomes easier, and gas exchange continues to improve.
Default progression: Gas exchange remains unstable, and close monitoring is still required.

Step 6
The patient is easier to ventilate, and gas exchange begins to improve. Which of the following should be recommended postadmission?

A. Admit to the ICU for continued ventilatory management and reassessment (+3)
B. Transfer to a low-acuity bed when SpO2 reaches 90% (-3)
C. Extubate and discharge after the next ABG (-3)
D. Observe in the emergency department without ICU admission (-3)

High-score progression: Final outcome: the patient is admitted to the ICU for ongoing ventilatory support and reassessment.
Default progression: Final outcome: delayed escalation leads to recurrent critical respiratory failure.
