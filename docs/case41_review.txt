Exhale Academy CSE Review
Case 41 -- Neonatal Critical (Delivery Room Apgar Resuscitation)

Intro
High-risk preterm delivery with poor respiratory effort requiring timed Apgar-based stabilization and reassessment.

Stem
Newborn has poor effort and cyanosis after delivery and requires rapid resuscitation decisions.

Step 1
A 30-week preterm infant is brought to the warmer immediately after delivery.

The following are noted:
HR 92/min
Weak respiratory effort
Poor color
SpO2 74%

Which of the following should be evaluated or performed initially? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 4).

A. Assess Apgar components, including respiratory effort and tone (+2)
B. Assess heart rate immediately (+2)
C. Provide thermal support and immediate stabilization at the warmer (+2)
D. Plan timed Apgar reassessment after intervention (+2)
E. Delay intervention until the full delivery-room history is complete (-3)
F. Transfer to routine care before stabilization (-3)

Revealed findings after indicated selections:
- The airway, tone, and respiratory effort should be scored as part of the Apgar assessment.
- Heart rate remains below 100/min.
- The infant needs thermal support and immediate respiratory stabilization.
- Apgar reassessment will need to continue at the appropriate interval after intervention.

Step 2
Findings suggest significant neonatal depression with ineffective respirations. Which of the following should be recommended FIRST?

A. Provide appropriate assisted ventilation and oxygen support based on the infant’s ineffective respirations (+3)
B. Observe without respiratory support because the infant is preterm (-3)
C. Begin chest compressions before correcting ventilation (-3)
D. Transfer to routine nursery care after the first improvement (-3)

Step 3
After initial respiratory support, the infant shows improving chest movement but still needs close reassessment.

Current findings are:
HR 118/min
SpO2 84%
Improved but still labored respirations

Which of the following should be evaluated now? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 4).

A. Repeat Apgar components at the correct interval (+2)
B. Trend oxygenation and work of breathing (+2)
C. Obtain objective gas-exchange data if distress remains significant (+2)
D. Determine the need for NICU-level post-resuscitation monitoring (+2)
E. Stop monitoring after one modest improvement (-3)
F. Delay reassessment for several hours (-3)

Revealed findings after indicated selections:
- Repeat Apgar components should be documented on time.
- Oxygenation trend and work of breathing still require close monitoring.
- ABG may help if respiratory distress remains significant.
- NICU-level post-resuscitation observation is still required.

Step 4
Which of the following should be recommended now?

A. Continue monitored NICU-level care with oxygen titration and post-resuscitation reassessment (+3)
B. Transfer to routine nursery care immediately (-3)
C. Discharge after temporary improvement (-3)
D. Observe without structured escalation triggers (-2)
