Exhale Academy CSE Review
Case 43 -- Neonatal Critical (Apnea of Prematurity Bradycardia Episodes)

Intro
Very preterm infant with recurrent apnea, bradycardia, and desaturation episodes requiring monitoring and escalation decisions.

Stem
Premature infant in the NICU has recurrent apnea spells with bradycardia and hypoxemia.

Step 1
A 29-week premature infant in the NICU has recurrent apnea alarms.

The following are noted:
HR 96/min
RR 22/min between episodes
SpO2 80%

Which of the following should be evaluated initially? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 4).

A. Assess apnea event frequency and whether bradycardia/desaturation accompany episodes (+2)
B. Confirm prematurity-related central apnea pattern (+2)
C. Assess airway patency and response to gentle stimulation (+2)
D. Obtain objective gas-exchange data if events are recurrent or severe (+2)
E. Delay stabilization until all diagnostics are complete (-3)
F. Assume every neonatal respiratory problem follows the same pathway (-3)

Revealed findings after indicated selections:
- Apnea episodes are accompanied by bradycardia and desaturation.
- The infant is very premature and not maintaining stable spontaneous drive.
- Airway patency and gentle stimulation response should be assessed.
- ABG: pH 7.31, PaCO2 52 torr, PaO2 46 torr, HCO3 26 mEq/L.

Step 2
Findings suggest apnea of prematurity with recurrent bradycardia/desaturation. Which of the following should be recommended FIRST?

A. Continue close monitoring, initiate caffeine therapy, and support ventilation/oxygenation as needed based on event burden (+3)
B. Use oxygen only and defer definitive disease-specific management (-3)
C. Delay escalation planning until prolonged deterioration occurs (-3)
D. Transfer to lower-acuity care after brief early improvement (-3)

Step 3
After initial treatment and close monitoring, the infant still has intermittent events.

Current findings are:
HR 104/min between episodes
RR 26/min between episodes
SpO2 88%

Which of the following should be evaluated now? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 4).

A. Trend apnea frequency and event severity (+2)
B. Trend heart-rate and oxygenation response during episodes (+2)
C. Determine whether noninvasive escalation is needed if events continue (+2)
D. Repeat blood gas if instability persists despite treatment (+2)
E. Stop close monitoring after one modest improvement (-3)
F. Delay reassessment for several hours (-3)

Revealed findings after indicated selections:
- Event frequency and severity still determine whether support is adequate.
- Heart-rate and oxygenation trends must continue to be tracked.
- Response to caffeine and need for noninvasive support should be reassessed.
- ABG: pH 7.33, PaCO2 49 torr, PaO2 52 torr, HCO3 25 mEq/L.

Step 4
Which of the following should be recommended now?

A. Continue NICU-level monitoring with apnea/bradycardia trend-based escalation triggers (+3)
B. Transfer to lower-acuity nursery care now (-3)
C. Discharge after temporary stabilization (-3)
D. Observe without explicit escalation triggers (-2)
