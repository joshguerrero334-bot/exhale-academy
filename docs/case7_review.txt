Exhale Academy CSE Review
Case 7 -- Sudden Hypoxemia With Hemodynamic Strain Pattern

Intro
Adult with abrupt pleuritic dyspnea, tachycardia, and worsening oxygenation requiring rapid stabilization and escalation decisions.

Stem
Acute cardiopulmonary decompensation with severe gas-exchange mismatch and worsening perfusion.

Step 1
A 46-year-old woman comes to the emergency department because of sudden right-sided pleuritic chest pain and severe dyspnea.

While breathing room air, the following are noted:
HR 126/min
RR 34/min
BP 98/62 mm Hg
SpO2 80%

She is anxious and diaphoretic.

Which of the following should be evaluated initially? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 3).

A. Breath sounds and symmetry of chest expansion (+2)
B. Pulse oximetry, heart rate, and blood pressure (+2)
C. Signs of right-heart strain and mental status (+2)
D. Routine pulmonary function testing (-3)
E. Exercise oximetry (-3)

High-score progression: Pleuritic chest pain and dyspnea persist, and hypoxemia remains severe.
Default progression: Assessment is incomplete. Hypoxemia worsens, and perfusion remains poor.

Step 2
Breath sounds are clear bilaterally. Neck veins are distended, and the patient remains tachycardic. Which of the following should be recommended FIRST?

A. Administer high-concentration oxygen and maintain close monitoring (+3)
B. Delay treatment until imaging is completed (-3)
C. Intubate immediately before any reassessment (-2)
D. Use low-flow oxygen by nasal cannula only (-2)

High-score progression: Oxygenation improves slightly, but tachycardia and dyspnea persist.
Default progression: Symptoms worsen, and blood pressure continues to fall.

Step 3
After oxygen therapy is started, the patient remains dyspneic.

While receiving O2 by nonrebreathing mask, the following are noted:
HR 122/min
RR 32/min
BP 92/58 mm Hg
SpO2 88%

ABG analysis reveals:
pH 7.47
PaCO2 31 torr
PaO2 56 torr
HCO3- 22 mEq/L

Which of the following should be evaluated now? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 3).

A. Oxygen saturation and blood pressure trend (+2)
B. Mental status and signs of worsening perfusion (+2)
C. ABG trend (+2)
D. Smoking-cessation counseling (-3)
E. Routine discharge planning (-3)

High-score progression: Hypoxemia and hypotension persist, and the patient remains high risk.
Default progression: Reassessment is delayed, and shock worsens.

Step 4
Blood pressure continues to fall, and hypoxemia persists. Which of the following should be recommended now?

A. Escalate to high-acuity management with immediate specialist coordination (+3)
B. Continue the same treatment and reassess later (-3)
C. Send the patient for routine testing before stabilization (-3)
D. Reduce oxygen because the patient is tachypneic (-3)

High-score progression: Perfusion improves slightly, and oxygenation stabilizes.
Default progression: Hypoxemia worsens, and hemodynamic instability increases.

Step 5
After escalation, oxygenation improves slightly and perfusion stabilizes. Which of the following should be evaluated or monitored now? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 3).

A. Continuous hemodynamic and oxygenation monitoring (+2)
B. Response to oxygen therapy and symptom trend (+2)
C. Coordination of definitive therapy pathway (+2)
D. Stop close monitoring after brief improvement (-3)
E. Transfer early to a low-acuity unit (-3)

High-score progression: Stabilization is maintained with ongoing high-acuity monitoring.
Default progression: Monitoring gaps leave a high risk of recurrent collapse.

Step 6
The patient remains high risk for recurrent instability. Which of the following should be recommended postadmission?

A. Admit to the ICU for continued respiratory and hemodynamic monitoring (+3)
B. Admit to an unmonitored floor bed (-3)
C. Discharge after temporary improvement (-3)
D. Observe without a defined escalation plan (-3)

High-score progression: Final outcome: the patient is admitted to the ICU for continued monitoring and definitive management.
Default progression: Final outcome: the level of care is inadequate, and recurrent instability occurs.
