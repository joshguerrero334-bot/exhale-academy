Exhale Academy CSE Review
Case 38 -- Pediatric Critical (Bronchiolitis RSV Edema/Apnea Risk)

Intro
Infant with bronchiolitis pattern, feeding difficulty, and escalating distress with apnea risk.

Stem
Infant has tachypnea, retractions, and worsening lower-airway distress after URI progression.

Step 1
A 7-month-old boy is brought to the emergency department because of worsening breathing after several days of cough and nasal congestion.

While receiving room air, the following are noted:
HR 162/min
RR 48/min
BP 92/54 mm Hg
SpO2 84%

Which of the following should be evaluated initially? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 4).

A. Auscultate breath sounds and assess retractions (+2)
B. Assess feeding tolerance and apnea history (+2)
C. Assess hydration status (+2)
D. Obtain an ABG if distress is significant (+2)
E. Delay stabilization while waiting for full diagnostics (-3)
F. Assume all wheezing children should receive the same treatment (-3)

Revealed findings after indicated selections:
- Breath sounds reveal diffuse crackles and wheezes.
- Feeding has decreased, and brief apnea episodes are reported.
- Hydration is poor with fewer wet diapers.
- ABG: pH 7.33, PaCO2 48 torr, PaO2 54 torr, HCO3 25 mEq/L.

Step 2
Findings suggest bronchiolitis with significant distress. Which of the following should be recommended FIRST?

A. Provide supportive care with oxygen and nasal suctioning, and escalate support only if distress or apnea worsens (+3)
B. Use oxygen only and defer definitive management (-3)
C. Apply routine bronchodilator and steroid escalation by default (-2)
D. Transfer to a lower-acuity setting after brief improvement (-3)

Step 3
Thirty minutes after initial treatment, the infant remains under close observation.

Current findings are:
HR 154/min
RR 44/min
BP 94/56 mm Hg
SpO2 90%

Which of the following should be evaluated now? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 4).

A. Reassess retractions, breath sounds, and oxygenation trend (+2)
B. Track hydration and apnea frequency (+2)
C. Determine whether escalation to higher-level respiratory support is needed (+2)
D. Avoid routine bronchodilator/steroid escalation if the pattern still fits bronchiolitis (+2)
E. Stop close monitoring after one modest improvement (-3)
F. Delay reassessment for several hours (-3)

Revealed findings after indicated selections:
- Retractions and breath sounds should be trended closely.
- Hydration status and apnea frequency still require close monitoring.
- Repeat oxygenation assessment determines whether higher-level support is needed.
- Routine bronchodilator or steroid escalation is still not indicated by default.

Step 4
Which of the following should be recommended now?

A. Continue monitored pediatric care with supportive therapy and escalation triggers for apnea or fatigue (+3)
B. Transfer to an unmonitored bed (-3)
C. Discharge after temporary improvement (-3)
D. Observe without explicit escalation triggers (-2)
