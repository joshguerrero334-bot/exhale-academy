Exhale Academy CSE Review
Case 10 -- Neuromuscular Ventilatory Failure Pattern

Intro
Adult with progressive generalized weakness presents with shallow breathing, poor cough, and rising CO2 risk requiring timely ventilatory support decisions.

Stem
Progressive ventilatory pump failure with impending hypercapnic decompensation.

Step 1
A 41-year-old woman with progressive generalized weakness comes to the emergency department because of increasing dyspnea and weak cough.

While breathing room air, the following are noted:
HR 108/min
RR 28/min
BP 142/86 mm Hg
SpO2 89%

She is speaking softly and taking shallow breaths.

Which of the following should be evaluated initially? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 3).

A. Cough strength and ability to clear secretions (+2)
B. Vital signs, pulse oximetry, and breathing pattern (+2)
C. Vital capacity, inspiratory force, and ABG analysis (+2)
D. Routine imaging before stabilization (-3)
E. Sedation before a ventilation plan is established (-3)

High-score progression: Shallow breathing and weak cough persist, and ventilatory weakness remains present.
Default progression: Assessment is incomplete. Ventilatory weakness worsens.

Step 2
Cough is weak, and inspiratory effort remains poor. Which of the following should be recommended FIRST?

A. Begin noninvasive ventilatory support with close reassessment (+3)
B. Use oxygen alone and reassess much later (-3)
C. Remove oxygen to assess baseline status (-3)
D. Proceed directly to intubation without further reassessment (-2)

High-score progression: Oxygenation improves slightly, but shallow breathing persists.
Default progression: Hypercapnic ventilatory failure progresses.

Step 3
After initial support, the patient remains tachypneic.

While receiving O2 by nasal cannula at 2 L/min, the following are noted:
HR 104/min
RR 30/min
BP 138/84 mm Hg
SpO2 91%

ABG analysis reveals:
pH 7.34
PaCO2 52 torr
PaO2 64 torr
HCO3- 27 mEq/L

Which of the following should be evaluated now? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 3).

A. Mental status and breathing pattern (+2)
B. ABG trend and repeat ventilatory mechanics (+2)
C. Secretion burden and cough effectiveness (+2)
D. Routine discharge planning (-3)
E. Stop monitoring after brief subjective improvement (-3)

High-score progression: PaCO2 rises, and inspiratory effort remains weak.
Default progression: Reassessment is delayed, and ventilatory failure worsens.

Step 4
PaCO2 rises further, and inspiratory effort weakens. Which of the following should be recommended now?

A. Proceed with controlled endotracheal intubation and mechanical ventilation (+3)
B. Continue the same noninvasive support despite worsening CO2 retention (-3)
C. Stop support and observe spontaneous effort (-3)
D. Transport for diagnostics before securing ventilation (-3)

High-score progression: The airway is secured, and gas exchange improves.
Default progression: Definitive ventilatory support is delayed, and instability increases.

Step 5
After escalation, the patient is receiving mechanical ventilation with improved oxygenation. Which of the following should be evaluated or managed now? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 3).

A. Ventilator settings and ABG response (+2)
B. Secretion-clearance effectiveness and airway patency (+2)
C. Continuous oxygenation and hemodynamic monitoring (+2)
D. Stop close monitoring after the first improved ABG (-3)
E. Transfer immediately to a low-acuity unit (-3)

High-score progression: Early ventilatory stabilization is maintained with close monitoring.
Default progression: Management gaps leave recurrent ventilatory deterioration risk.

Step 6
The patient remains at risk for recurrent ventilatory failure. Which of the following should be recommended postadmission?

A. Admit to the ICU for continued ventilatory support and reassessment (+3)
B. Transfer to an unmonitored floor bed (-3)
C. Discharge after temporary improvement (-3)
D. Observe without a defined escalation plan (-3)

High-score progression: Final outcome: the patient is admitted to the ICU for continued ventilatory support and reassessment.
Default progression: Final outcome: the level of care is inadequate, and recurrent ventilatory failure occurs.
