Exhale Academy CSE Review
Case 2 -- Term Infant Transition Failure (Meconium Context)

Intro
Term infant delivered through meconium-stained fluid with poor tone and ineffective respirations requiring immediate delivery-room stabilization.

Stem
Depressed term newborn requires rapid airway, ventilation, and reassessment decisions at the warmer.

Step 1
A term male infant is brought to the warmer immediately after delivery through thick meconium-stained fluid.

The following are noted:
HR 84/min
Weak respiratory effort
Poor tone
SpO2 62%

Which of the following should be evaluated or performed initially? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 4).

A. Position the airway and assess chest movement (+2)
B. Assess heart rate and breathing effectiveness immediately (+2)
C. Assess tone and respiratory effort (+2)
D. Recognize that routine deep tracheal suctioning before ventilation is not automatic (+2)
E. Delay intervention until a complete physical exam is finished (-3)
F. Start compressions before correcting ventilation (-3)

Revealed findings after indicated selections:
- The airway is not well positioned, and chest movement is minimal.
- Heart rate remains below 100/min after the initial quick assessment.
- The infant is not crying effectively and has poor tone.
- Routine deep tracheal suctioning before ventilation is not automatically indicated.

Step 2
Heart rate remains below 100/min and respirations are ineffective. Which of the following should be recommended FIRST?

A. Begin positive-pressure ventilation and confirm effective chest movement (+3)
B. Use blow-by oxygen only (-3)
C. Begin chest compressions immediately without ventilation correction (-3)
D. Transfer to routine nursery care and reassess later (-3)

Step 3
Positive-pressure ventilation is started, but chest rise is poor.

Current findings are:
HR 78/min
SpO2 66%

Which of the following should be evaluated or corrected now? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 4).

A. Reposition the head and improve mask seal (+2)
B. Clear mouth and nose secretions if they are obstructing ventilation (+2)
C. Increase inspiratory pressure enough to obtain chest rise (+2)
D. Recognize that effective ventilation should improve heart rate before compressions are started (+2)
E. Pause ventilation for diagnostic blood sampling (-3)
F. Continue ineffective breaths unchanged (-3)

Revealed findings after indicated selections:
- Head position and mask seal need correction.
- Secretions in the mouth and nose may be limiting airflow.
- More inspiratory pressure is required to achieve chest rise.
- Effective ventilation improves heart rate before compressions are considered.

Step 4
After effective ventilation, heart rate rises above 100/min, but respiratory distress persists. Which of the following should be recommended now?

A. Admit to monitored neonatal care with oxygen titration, glucose/temperature monitoring, and continued respiratory reassessment (+3)
B. Transfer immediately to routine well-baby care (-3)
C. Discharge after brief observation (-3)
D. Observe without structured monitoring (-2)
