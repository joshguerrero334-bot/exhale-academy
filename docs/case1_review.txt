Case 1 -- Practice Layout Audit (Upper Airway)

Step 1
A 43-year-old man arrives with rapidly worsening noisy breathing after a day of severe throat pain.

While breathing room air, the following are noted:
HR 110/min
RR 30/min
BP 142/96 mm Hg
SpO2 86%

Which of the following should be evaluated initially? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 4).

Options
A. Auscultate for stridor
B. Assess the ability to speak
C. Assess the voice and throat pain history
D. Review the CBC
E. Review a portable lateral neck radiograph only if it can be obtained without destabilizing the airway
F. Force the patient supine for a complete oral examination
G. Delay all treatment until imaging is complete

Step 2
The findings suggest an upper-airway emergency with high risk of abrupt obstruction. Which of the following should be recommended FIRST?

Options
A. Maintain the patient upright, provide humidified oxygen, and proceed with controlled airway planning with anesthesia and ENT backup
B. Observe on oxygen alone without a definitive airway plan
C. Use heavy sedation before the airway is secured
D. Transport for CT before airway control

Step 3
While the airway team is assembling, oxygenation remains marginal.

While receiving humidified O2, the following are noted:
HR 114/min
RR 32/min
BP 146/98 mm Hg
SpO2 88%

Which of the following should be evaluated now? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 4).

Options
A. Reassess work of breathing and fatigue
B. Obtain an ABG
C. Trend continuous pulse oximetry
D. Confirm that a backup surgical-airway plan is ready
E. Stop reassessment after a brief improvement in oxygen saturation
F. Delay airway planning until the next routine cycle

Step 4
Oxygenation and fatigue worsen while the upper-airway obstruction risk remains high. Which of the following should be recommended now?

Options
A. Proceed with definitive airway control in a controlled setting with full backup available
B. Continue the same therapy unchanged and reassess much later
C. Transfer to a low-acuity bed
