Exhale Academy CSE Review
Case 47 -- Neonatal Critical (CDH Surgical Emergency Mediastinal Shift)

Intro
Neonate with severe respiratory distress and classic congenital diaphragmatic hernia clues requiring immediate airway and decompression decisions.

Stem
Newborn with severe distress and asymmetric chest findings requires immediate CDH-specific management.

Step 1
A term newborn develops severe respiratory distress immediately after birth.

The following are noted:
HR 170/min
RR 64/min
SpO2 74%

Which of the following should be evaluated initially? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 4).

A. Assess unilateral breath sounds and chest movement (+2)
B. Assess whether the abdomen is scaphoid (+2)
C. Assess cardiac sound displacement and severity of distress (+2)
D. Review radiographic evidence if available without delaying stabilization (+2)
E. Begin routine bag-mask ventilation while sorting out the diagnosis (-3)
F. Delay stabilization until imaging is complete (-3)

Revealed findings after indicated selections:
- Breath sounds are markedly reduced on the left.
- The abdomen appears scaphoid.
- Cardiac sounds are displaced to the right.
- Chest radiograph supports bowel in the thorax with mediastinal shift.

Step 2
Findings suggest congenital diaphragmatic hernia. Which of the following should be recommended FIRST?

A. Proceed with endotracheal intubation, place an OG tube for decompression, and avoid bag-mask ventilation (+3)
B. Use bag-mask ventilation and observe the response (-3)
C. Use oxygen only and delay definitive airway management (-3)
D. Transfer to lower-acuity care after brief stabilization (-3)

Step 3
After initial CDH-specific stabilization, the infant remains critically ill.

Current findings are:
HR 156/min
SpO2 84%
EtCO2 50 mm Hg

Which of the following should be evaluated now? SELECT AS MANY AS INDICATED (MAX 4).

A. Confirm endotracheal tube position and adequacy of ventilation (+2)
B. Reassess OG decompression and abdominal/chest distention (+2)
C. Repeat blood-gas assessment and trend ventilation/oxygenation (+2)
D. Determine ongoing NICU and surgical readiness (+2)
E. Stop close monitoring after slight saturation improvement (-3)
F. Return to bag-mask ventilation if oxygenation remains low (-3)

Revealed findings after indicated selections:
- Endotracheal tube position and effective ventilation must be confirmed.
- OG decompression effectiveness should be reassessed.
- ABG: pH 7.27, PaCO2 54 torr, PaO2 52 torr, HCO3 24 mEq/L.
- Ongoing surgical/NICU readiness is still required.

Step 4
Which of the following should be recommended now?

A. Continue NICU-level care with ventilatory support, gastric decompression, and urgent surgical planning (+3)
B. Transfer to routine nursery care (-3)
C. Discharge after temporary stabilization (-3)
D. Observe without explicit escalation or surgical planning (-2)
